About Missoula

Missoula, known as the "Garden City," is a vibrant university town nestled in the heart of the northern Rockies. With its rich cultural scene, outdoor recreational opportunities, and natural beauty, Missoula offers a unique Montana experience.

Key Information

  • Population (2020): Approximately 75,000
  • Region: Western Montana
  • Median Household Income: $59,783
  • Incorporated: March 8, 1883

Education and Culture

Missoula is home to the University of Montana, which significantly influences the city's cultural and economic landscape. The city is known for its thriving arts scene, outdoor enthusiasm, and environmental consciousness.

Attractions

  1. University of Montana campus
  2. Clark Fork River Park
  3. Missoula Art Museum
  4. Mount Sentinel and the "M" Trail
  5. Carousel for Missoula

Did You Know?

Missoula is often referred to as the "Garden City" due to its mild winters relative to the rest of Montana.
