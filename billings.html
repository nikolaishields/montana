About Billings

Billings, known as the "Magic City," is Montana's largest city and a hub of commerce, culture, and outdoor recreation. Nestled in the shadow of the Rimrocks, Billings offers a unique urban experience in the heart of Big Sky Country.

Key Information

  • Population (2020): 117,116
  • Region: Southern Montana
  • Median Household Income: $59,656
  • Incorporated: March 24, 1882

Economy and Culture

Billings serves as a major trade and distribution center for much of Montana and northern Wyoming. The city boasts a diverse economy, with strengths in healthcare, energy, agriculture, and tourism.

Attractions

  1. Pictograph Cave State Park
  2. ZooMontana
  3. Yellowstone Art Museum
  4. Moss Mansion Historic House Museum

Did You Know?

Billings was nicknamed the "Magic City" because of its rapid growth from its founding as a railroad town in 1882.
